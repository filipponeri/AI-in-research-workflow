What this workshop is

AI in Research Workflow Foundations is an online intensive and interactive workshop where you build and share your own AI in research workflow, experiment with it and learn from the other participants' experience. The workshop is based on the philosophy of learning-by-doing, learning-by-experimenting, and learning-by-sharing. Active participation, assignment completion before each session, experience sharing, and thoughtful feedback to other participants' developing AI workflows is essential. All in five consecutive weekly sessions.

The workshop is free. There is no equity ask, no upsell, and no commercial angle for participants.

The workshop is a unique learning opportunity and it is not an entertainment activity where one sit back and watch the action unfold. If a participant cannot keep up with the workshop pace and required active participation, they will not be invited to the following session. Indeed active participants will not benefit in any way from talking to an unresponsive computer screen.

Each session will be held if the majority of active participants to the previous session vote for the session to take place. Thus signalling that they are feeling the on-going benefit from the workshop continuation. At the end of each session, each participant can provide feedback on others' active participation or lack of it. The organizer will make the final decision to not invite further participants who are not contributing to the communal learning.

Who it is for

  • You completed at least one year of your Marie Curie fellowship or of your PhD.
  • You already have published at least one paper with your significant individual contribution.
  • You have a solid grasp of how the manual research workflow for your research field operates. And you are interested in experimenting with an LLM of your choosing (chatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.) to automatize part of your research workflow.
  • You can attend Wednesday 18:00–20:00 CET for five consecutive weeks from September 10
  • You commit to follow the workshop learning philosophy, and to complete the assignments before each session.
  • At each session, you are prepared to share your learnings from the assignments.

Frequently asked questions

Do I have to pay to attend the workshop?

No. The workshop is free. No fee. You will not be asked to join any program, fund, or organization as a condition of participating.

Do I to know how the traditional research workflow is done in my field? Do I need to have published at least one paper to prove my research experience?

Yes, you do. To both questions.

What if I do not complete the assignments given before a session or if I miss the one day before the session deadline to mail in my assignments? What if I miss a session? What if I do not feel like to actively participate in the session conversation?

In this case, you will be excluded from attending the remaining sessions. Each assignment and each sesssion feeds directly into the next ones. If your schedule does not allow you to commit the time and attention for attending the workshop, please do not apply. Given that the 5th session requires every participant to share in advance their developed research workflow and their findings with the other participants in order to gather their feedback, if you are not developing your AI powered research workflow during the workshop, you will not gain any learning nor will you contribute to the other participants' learning.

What if I discover that AI is a bad thing for my research workflow and I do not want to continue participating in the workshop?

This would be very good learning. You would be welcome to share your negative findings, if you like, and to stop attending the workshop.

Will I get a participation certificate after completing the workshop?

No, you will not. That would be a vanity token. You should be here because you want to experiment with AI and your research workflow, not because you want to add another paragraph to your cv.

Can I get some slack during the workshop period because I have to complete my paper, apply for my grant proposal, I'm in holiday, I need to spend time with my family, etc.?

No. If you learn that you have more pressing matters to do than keeping up with the workshop set of activities, it means that now is not a good time for you to create a startup, This clarity is an excellent outcome provided by the workshop. It is very unlikely that your startup will get funded if you do not make a priority of your startup. Yes, you will have less time to spend with your family and friends or to write your next paper or grant application. This is experiencing the opportunity cost in action.

My fellowship was more than 10 years ago. Can I still apply?

Yes, there is no upper limit. The one year minimum exists to ensure participants have had time to experience and think about the question: 'who do I want to become now?'. Choosing an entrepreneurial path is an answer, choosing an academic career is another one.

I am a current MC fellow / PhD student, or I completed my fellowship/phd less than one year ago. Can I still apply?

No, you cannot apply. The one year minimum exists to ensure participants have had time to decide they really want to become a startup founder.

Can I keep my camera off during the sessions because... (fill up at your pleasure)?

No. If you cannot commit time and attention when other participants share their findings, it means that you have other priorities.
